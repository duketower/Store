# How Much Does a Business Website Cost in India?

If you have ever asked a developer for a quote and received a number that felt random — you are not alone. Website pricing in India varies wildly, and without context it is almost impossible to know whether a quote is fair.

This guide breaks down what different types of business websites actually cost, what drives those costs, and what to watch out for before you sign anything.

---

## Why Pricing Varies So Much

A website is not a single product. It is a combination of design, development, content, and ongoing maintenance — and each of those can be done cheaply, properly, or expensively.

The main variables that determine price:

- **Type of website** — brochure site, service website, e-commerce store, or custom web app
- **Who builds it** — freelancer, agency, or template platform
- **Complexity** — number of pages, custom features, integrations
- **Content** — whether writing and photography are included
- **Location of the team** — metro vs tier-2 city pricing differences are real

---

## Common Website Types and Their Price Ranges

### 1. Brochure Website (3–6 pages)

Best for: local service businesses, clinics, consultants, shops

**What you get:**
- Home, About, Services, Contact
- Mobile-friendly design
- Basic SEO setup

**Typical cost in India:** ₹15,000 – ₹50,000

**Watch out for:**
- Templates sold as custom work
- No CMS — you cannot update content yourself
- No post-launch support

---

### 2. Business Service Website (6–15 pages)

Best for: agencies, professional firms, companies with multiple services

**What you get:**
- Full service and team pages
- Blog or insights section
- Enquiry forms
- Google Analytics
- On-page SEO

**Typical cost in India:** ₹40,000 – ₹1,50,000

**Watch out for:**
- Vague deliverables in the contract
- Shared hosting that slows down in peak traffic
- No clear ownership of the domain and hosting

---

### 3. E-Commerce Website

Best for: product businesses wanting to sell online directly

**What you get:**
- Product catalogue
- Cart and payment gateway
- Order management
- Basic inventory

**Typical cost in India:** ₹60,000 – ₹3,00,000+

**Watch out for:**
- Payment gateway setup fees and annual charges
- Platforms that lock your data
- Hidden transaction fees

---

### 4. Custom Web App

Best for: businesses that need a system — not just a website

**What you get:**
- Built-to-spec functionality
- User accounts, dashboards, workflows
- API integrations
- Scalable architecture

**Typical cost in India:** ₹1,50,000 – ₹10,00,000+

**Watch out for:**
- Scope creep without a change-control process
- No documentation or handover plan
- Single developer with no backup

---

## What Is Usually Not Included in the Quote

Most website quotes do not cover:

- **Domain registration** — ₹700–₹1,500/year depending on extension
- **Hosting** — ₹2,000–₹10,000/year for shared hosting; more for VPS or cloud
- **SSL certificate** — often free with modern hosts, but confirm
- **Content writing** — most developers do not write copy
- **Photography or video** — stock photos are sometimes included, custom shoots are not
- **Annual maintenance** — updates, security patches, small changes

Always ask for a full cost-of-ownership breakdown before committing.

---

## Checklist: Questions to Ask Before Paying

Before you pay any developer or agency, get clear answers to these:

- [ ] Who owns the domain after the project is done?
- [ ] Who owns the hosting account?
- [ ] Can I update content myself, or do I need to call you?
- [ ] What happens if I need a change after launch?
- [ ] Is SEO setup included, or is that a separate cost?
- [ ] What does post-launch support cost per month?
- [ ] Will the site pass Google's Core Web Vitals checks?
- [ ] Do you provide a written scope of work before starting?

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I get a good website for under ₹20,000?**

Yes, if your needs are simple — a clean 3-page brochure site with basic SEO is achievable. Be realistic about what you will and will not get at that budget.

**Is a cheaper website always bad?**

Not always. A well-executed template site from a reliable developer beats a bloated custom build that was never maintained. Price matters less than whether the person building it is accountable and knows what they are doing.

**Should I use Wix or Squarespace instead?**

For very small businesses with no technical support, template platforms can work. But you give up control, performance, and customisation. Most businesses with real growth plans outgrow them quickly.

**How do I know if I am being overcharged?**

Get two or three quotes. Ask each developer to explain what is in scope. Vague answers or huge price gaps without explanation are warning signs.
