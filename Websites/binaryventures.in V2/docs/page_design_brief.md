# Binary Ventures Website V2 Page Design Brief

This document translates the approved strategy and copy into design responsibilities for each page.

## Shared Site Rules

- Every page must feel part of one premium system
- Header and footer are persistent across the site
- The visual style should be bold premium tech, but not noisy or gimmick-heavy
- Layout should feel deliberate, high-contrast, and spacious
- Every page must make the next action obvious without overloading the user

## Home

### Primary Job

Create trust quickly, communicate what Binary Ventures does, and move serious visitors toward Work, Services, or Contact.

### Design Goals

- strong first-screen authority
- immediate clarity around the offer
- confidence without visual chaos
- proof introduced early

### Required Sections

1. Hero
2. Positioning statement
3. Service pillars preview
4. Proof preview
5. Process preview
6. CTA close

### Design Notes

- hero should feel premium and assertive, not decorative for its own sake
- the first scroll should immediately deepen understanding, not repeat the hero
- proof preview should feel like a serious entry into the Work page, not a carousel of random tiles

## Work

### Primary Job

Prove capability through anonymized but credible case-study framing.

### Design Goals

- make the work feel real and substantial
- avoid looking like a generic portfolio gallery
- create trust through structure and clarity

### Required Sections

1. Page intro
2. Featured case studies
3. Capability coverage / solution types
4. Work philosophy or delivery standards
5. CTA

### Design Notes

- case studies should feel like business problems solved, not gallery cards
- anonymization must still feel premium and intentional
- this page should likely be the strongest page after Home

## Services

### Primary Job

Explain the offer clearly without flattening the company into a list of disconnected services.

### Design Goals

- preserve the umbrella offer of complete business tech setup
- show breadth without feeling vague
- make packaged and custom engagement both feel valid

### Required Sections

1. Intro
2. Framing statement
3. Service pillars
4. Engagement models
5. Pricing-ready area for later
6. CTA

### Design Notes

- the page should feel structured, not salesy
- service blocks should support real business copy, not only short headlines

## About

### Primary Job

Build trust through operating style, founder-led accountability, and company character.

### Design Goals

- human, but still premium
- founder-led, but not self-promotional
- calm confidence

### Required Sections

1. Page intro
2. Founder-led model
3. Working style
4. Values / standards
5. CTA

### Design Notes

- the page should make the studio feel serious and trustworthy
- avoid clichés like startup culture language or inspirational filler

## Contact

### Primary Job

Make it easy for a serious prospect to reach out.

### Design Goals

- low friction
- calm confidence
- very clear next action

### Required Sections

1. Contact intro
2. Book a call route
3. Email route
4. Optional form or enquiry framing

### Design Notes

- this page should feel concise and premium
- too many fields or too many options would weaken it
